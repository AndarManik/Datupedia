My Journey with Datupedia

The concept of Datupedia was born from a simple yet profound realization: the way backlinks frame an idea is as crucial as the definition of the idea itself. This mirrors the linguistic principle where a word is often defined by its context. Imagine learning a new word by seeing it in various texts; similarly, understanding an idea through its interconnected references offers a rich, multifaceted understanding. This sparked my curiosity about visualizing this web of backlinks to offer a deeper grasp of any given topic.

My initial assumption was that accessing this backlink information would be straightforward, perhaps through a readily available API, followed by integrating this data with ChatGPT to craft comprehensive articles. However, the journey was filled with unforeseen challenges. First, I realized that Wikipedia did not provide a simple method to extract text containing specific links, prompting me to develop a custom parser. Second, the vast amount of text exceeded the language model's context window, leading me to devise an efficient sampling method. Finally, the format of the output was more critical than anticipated, necessitating a minimal formatting approach and an additional parsing stage to ensure clarity and coherence.

The final iteration of Datupedia is much more than a web tool; it is the embodiment of numerous design decisions and innovative solutions. It offers a unique window into the intricate web of backlinks on any Wikipedia page, providing users with an enriched perspective. By seamlessly blending advanced AI with intuitive design, Datupedia stands as a testament to the power of technology in redefining how we explore and understand information. It's not just about accessing information; it's about experiencing the interconnectedness of knowledge in a way that is both insightful and transformative.
